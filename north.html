Mujica de Peixe

The cuisine of the northern region of the country is very marked by dishes with indigenous influences, by the consumption of fish and fruits typical of the place, coming from the immense natural wealth of the Amazon. One of the most consumed dishes in all the northern states is Mujica de Peixe, which consists of roasted fish (tambaqui, preferably) in a stew rich in spices, thickened with flour and served with white rice or some other side dish. The dish of indigenous origin is widely consumed by the riverside population of the region, and the idea is always to reuse a fish that was roasted for a previous meal.

How to make

  1. Shred the tambaqui, removing the bones and set aside
  2. Heat the oil and brown the onion
  3. Add tomato, murupi pepper, paprika and sauté.
  4. Add the shredded fish and season with salt
  5. Add boiling water and chopped cilantro
  6. Season with chicory and basil
  7. Boil 10 minutes
  8. Lower the heat and add the flour little by little
  9. Stir until thickened
  10. Remove from heat and serve in gourds or consomme bowls
