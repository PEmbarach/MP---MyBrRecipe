Feijão Tropeiro

As a region that received intense activities in the period of colonization of Brazil, the Southeast is today a great mix of cultures. Many dishes have a Portuguese influence, but it is also impossible to deny Italian, Japanese, Arab and African traits in the typical cuisine of the region – the latter, in fact, are also found throughout the country. Some say that feijoada, for example, came from the influence of slaves who lived in the Southeast.
Each of its states has typical dishes, but one of the tastiest in the region is the famous tropeiro beans, which contain a mixture of beans, manioc flour, sausage, crackling, garlic, onions, eggs and other seasonings. The origin of the name dates back to the period when the “tropeiros” – men who transported goods by troops – gathered to eat and invented this dish that took all the ingredients they had most access to at the time. To this day, the dish remains a strong element of the tradition of São Paulo and Minas Gerais.

How to make

  1. Cook the beans and reserve. Fry the eggs and reserve. Fry the bacon and set aside. In the same pan that you fried the egg and bacon, add the oil, garlic, onion and salt.
  2. Saute the beans, letting it boil for about 5 minutes. Add the cassava flour little by little. After ready, put the bacon and lastly the eggs and the green smell.
